Preserving Family Hierlooms

A presentation I compiled to present to the general public on how to preserve material culture artifacts. It included pictoral examples of material to help them identify material breakdown, defining terms used in preservation, and suggestions for proper storage material. I created partnerships in the community to enrich the experience through donations of archival material for the attendees to take home.

This presentation was given in November 2019.

Skeptical Gaze: How Photomontage Blurs the Lines of Reality

This exhibit centers around the idea of applying skepticism to visual imagery regardless of the era or age of the photographs. Techniques that manipulate images, such as photomontage, have been extensively used throughout the modern analog film photographic process and continue to be used in a prolific capacity within the digital photography realm with programs like Adobe Photoshop. This exhibition specifically connects contemporary ideas about skepticism towards visual imagery with traditional darkroom techniques as a way to encourage the audience to assess their trust and belief in what visual representations they are consuming. Skeptical Gaze: How Photomontage Blurs the Lines of Reality is an arrangement of photographs that use both fine art photography and mass media produced photography as a vehicle to begin this conversation.

This exhibition opened for Fall 2019 at Syracuse University Art Galleries. Find the information here.

Integrating Language Learning in Museums: Developing Cultural Tolerance in an Increasingly Diverse Society

This research centers around the notion and implmentation of foreign language integration in cultural heritage institutions through exhibition design. For decades foreign language education in American formal school settings that has seen a consistent decline in both public and private schools of all levels. However, language transcends all cultures and relates to all physical objects and ideas, exposure to new languages should be emphasized as a crucial aspect of education, access, and increasing cultural tolerance in a diverse society.

This research was presented at the 2019 Popular Culture Association/American Culture Association's Annual Conference and the 2019 Museum Association of New York's Annual Conference.

The Power of After-Hours: Young Adult Engagement and Programming in Museums

This project explores the cultivation of an ever deepening relationship between young adult audiences (ages 21-40) and museums and cultural institutions through the use of special event programming. Museums are turning to after-hours events to capture this audience’s attention, developing them as a mode of communication to solidify connections between the institution and young adults. In this research I draw on three case studies in the Rochester, New York area and conduct data analysis of guest surveys provided by the Rochester Museum and Science Center to create recommendations for museums and other cultural institutions.
